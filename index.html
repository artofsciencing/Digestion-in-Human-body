Types of Heterotrophic Nutrition

Heterotrophs obtain organic nutrients from other organisms. Three main strategies exist in nature — humans practise holozoic nutrition.

🍽️
Type 01
Holozoic
Whole food is ingested, digested internally, absorbed, and indigestible remains egested. This is the mode of nutrition in all animals. Food undergoes both mechanical and chemical digestion inside a specialised gut.
Examples: Humans · Mammals · Birds · Reptiles · Fish · Amoeba
🍄
Type 02
Saprophytic (Saprotrophic)
Organisms secrete digestive enzymes onto dead or decaying organic matter outside the body (extracellular digestion), then absorb the soluble products. These organisms are the planet's primary decomposers, cycling nutrients back into soil.
Examples: Mushrooms · Moulds · Most bacteria · Yeast
🦠
Type 03
Parasitic
The organism (parasite) lives on or inside a host and feeds at the host's expense, often causing harm. Many parasites have simplified digestive systems or none at all — they absorb pre-digested nutrients directly from host tissues.
Examples: Tapeworm · Liver fluke · Plasmodium · Mistletoe · Ticks

The Five Processes of
Holozoic Nutrition

Click each stage to expand. These five steps transform a meal into cellular energy.

👄
Step 01
Ingestion
Taking food into the body through the mouth. In humans, this involves biting, chewing, and swallowing. The tongue, lips, cheeks, and saliva all cooperate to form a bolus (ball of food) ready for swallowing.
⚙️
Step 02
Digestion
Mechanical: Physical breakdown — chewing (mastication), churning in the stomach, segmentation in the intestine. Increases surface area for enzymes.

Chemical: Enzymes hydrolyse macromolecules. Carbohydrates → monosaccharides. Proteins → amino acids. Lipids → glycerol + fatty acids.
🔼
Step 03
Absorption
Digested nutrients pass through the wall of the small intestine into the blood (glucose, amino acids) or lymph (fatty acids, glycerol). Villi and microvilli increase the absorptive surface area to approximately 200 m² — the size of a tennis court.
🔬
Step 04
Assimilation
Absorbed nutrients are incorporated into the body's own molecules. Glucose is used in cellular respiration or stored as glycogen/fat. Amino acids build proteins. Fatty acids form cell membranes. This is truly "you are what you eat."
🚽
Step 05
Egestion
Undigested material (mostly fibre, dead cells, bacteria) is compacted in the large intestine, water is reabsorbed, and the remaining faeces are expelled via the rectum and anus. This is NOT excretion — faeces were never absorbed into the body.

Digestive Enzymes

Enzymes are biological catalysts that hydrolyse (break by adding water) large molecules into absorbable monomers. Each enzyme is substrate-specific.

Absorption in the
Small Intestine

The ileum is adapted for maximum absorption: villi, microvilli (brush border), good blood supply, thin walls, and a very long length.

What gets absorbed, and how?
Different nutrients use different transport mechanisms across the intestinal epithelium and into the circulation.
Glucose & Fructose
→ Blood capillaries → Hepatic portal vein → Liver
Active transport (co-transported with Na⁺) and facilitated diffusion via GLUT transporters.
Amino Acids
→ Blood capillaries → Hepatic portal vein → Liver
Active transport via specific amino acid carrier proteins in the brush border membrane.
Fatty Acids & Glycerol
→ Reassembled into chylomicrons → Lacteal → Lymphatic system → Blood
Simple diffusion across membrane; re-synthesised into triglycerides inside epithelial cells, packaged as chylomicrons, secreted into lacteals.
Water & Minerals
Small intestine (90%) + Large intestine (remainder)
Osmosis (water follows solute gradients). Minerals (Na⁺, Ca²⁺, Fe²⁺) via specific channels and active transport.
Vitamins
Fat-soluble (A,D,E,K) → lacteal; Water-soluble (B,C) → blood
Fat-soluble vitamins dissolve in micelles and enter with fats. Water-soluble vitamins diffuse directly into blood capillaries.
